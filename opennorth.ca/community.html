Community & Lab Projects

Open North is an active participant in the open government and open data communities. We're passionate about making democracy better through increased accesss to government information and civic engagement. In addition to our core projects and services, we're active members in a handful of groups and committees which strive for similar ideals. Our staff are also often invited to speak on topics related to open government data standards and online civic engagment.

To learn more about our work in the community, join our mailing list for updates

Groups and Committees

  • Table de concertation sur les données ouvertes de Montréal
    Open North joined the Table de concertation in April of 2012. The purpose of the Table is to discuss the evolution of open data in Montréal, with 6 members from the City and 6 from the civil society. As an active member of the table, Open North has submitted lists of datasets that should be released and has proposed changes to the way the open data policy is followed by the city.

  • W3C Government Linked Data (GLD) Working Group
    Open North's Executive Director, James McKinney, joined this working group as an invited expert. The GLD group works to develop standards which help governments publish their data as effective and usable Linked Data, using Semantic Web technologies.

Open North Labs

We're always looking for new ways to serve our mission to increase access to government information and increase civic engagement. Here we've listed some of our smaller projects, which we call "Open North Labs"

VeloPlan is a bike trip planner with a twist: it can also plan a trip with the bike-share system available in Montréal. VéloPlan is also able to change your trip based on your prefence: flatness, speed or security, you decide and VeloPlan gives you the best option. Véloplan is a fork of the Cibi.me open source software developed by OpenPlans and relies on the Open Trip Planner instance for Montréal

Open Trip Planner for Montreal is an open source multimodal trip planner. Open North supported and pursued the work of Nicolas Saunier and Guillaume Barreau to set up a complete instance of Open Trip Planner for the Montreal metropolitan area. Open Trip Planner support trip planning with all the transit agencies of the region, as well as walk, bike, bike-share and combinations of several of these transportation modes, for example walk + bike share + public transportation.

Yul311 is an example of a web interface option for 311 service in Montréal. The 311 service is used by many cities as the one-stop shop phone number to declare any non-urgent issue like broken lights, grafittis or potholes. In order to make 311 services more efficient and accessible, Open311, an open standard, has been designed in 2009 to submit request via the web. Open311 has been adopted by several large cities like Chicago, San Francisco or Toronto. Yul311 uses open source software developed by the City of Bloomington, Indiana (namely uReport and Open311 Proxy and some custom code) to demonstrate the usefulness of Open311 in Montréal.
