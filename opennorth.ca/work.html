Civic Engagement

Citizen Budget is a powerful online budget simulator that involves residents in decision-making processes and demonstrates a municipality's commitment to citizen engagement. First launched in the Plateau Mont-Royal borough of Montreal, QC in 2011, Citizen Budget has been deployed by municipalities across North America.

The MyCityHall.ca platform will allow citizens to track the activity of local elected officials, motions, agendas, and give citizens an opportunity to ask questions, and discuss events from city hall.The platform is currently being developed into an open standard so that civic-minded developers around the world can adapt it to track their local government. MyCityHall.ca will launch in summer 2013 in Montreal, Toronto and Ottawa

Open Government Data

Open 511 is an open standard and framework for managing and publishing road events, such as road work, hazards, accidents and closures. The API is currently available as a draft. This project is funded by the Government of Canada.

Represent is an open database of elected representatives and administrative boundaries for the federal, provincial and municipal levels of government in Canada. Represent is deployed by over a dozen civil society organizations to connect members and volunteers to their representatives.

Gazette Documents is a database of contracts awarded by the City of Montreal. Built for the Montreal Gazette, it extracts contracts data from hundreds of PDFs so that journalists and citizens can explore and analyze how public money is spent.

Retired Projects

Resto-Net makes it easy for Montrealers to check if a local restaurant has recently violated a health code.

Patiner Montreal publishes the up-to-date conditions of outdoor rinks in Montreal to help skaters plan their winter fun.
