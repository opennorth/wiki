Our Mission

Open North is a Canadian non-profit that creates online tools to educate and empower citizens to participate actively in Canadian democracy. We develop tools for civil society and governments to reduce the barriers to effective participation. We do this with two main strategies:

Improve access to government information:

  • make it easily available, by pulling it out of dark corners of government websites, taking it out of PDFs and making access to information requests
  • make it accessible, by providing context to numbers, explaining legislative jargon and using language everyone understands
  • make it relevant, by letting citizens find information specific to their community, interests and situation

Make participation easy, fun, and meaningful

  • work with governments to design new participatory processes and to ensure citizen’s voices are heard
  • increase the number of ways citizens can engage with elected officials and government information
  • help citizens connect with each other and organize around issues they care about

How do we do this?

  • We collect and contextualize information that citizens need to participate effectively
  • We provide free platforms for citizens to connect with each other and their elected officials
  • We create open standards for publishing and sharing government information
  • We help other organizations understand what open information can do for them
  • We are a non-partisan organization that encourages the reuse of best practices
  • We believe in cooperation: our projects are open source and we collaborate on other projects in our sector and community

Who do we work with?

Our projects have a wide variety of uses but are most commonly deployed by government, the media, and civil society organizations looking to create meaningful exchanges with citizens. Some examples of organizations we’ve recently worked with include:

  • Plateau Mont-Royal, Quebec
  • Town of Newmarket, Ontario
  • Mankato, Minnesota
  • Langley, British Columbia
  • The Montreal Gazette
  • The Sunlight Foundation
  • mySociety
  • Participatory Politics Foundation
  • The David Suzuki Foundation
  • Environmental Defense Canada
  • Dogwood Initiative

Who funds Open North?

Open North is a federal nonprofit funded through grants, sponsorships, awards, and individual donations. We often engage in contracts with government and civil society organizations to strengthen democratic activity through open initiatives. As a social enterprise, we license our Citizen Budget service to municipalities like Newmarket, Ontario and engage in contracts with media groups such as the Montreal Gazette. We have received funding from the Government of Canada, Google Montreal, Société de développement économique Ville-Marie, and the Institut du Nouveau Monde.
