Standards Development

Open North is dedicated to establishing open data standards that increase access to government information and promote interoperability. Open North participates in the development of several open data specifications, in addition to its own Popolo and Open511 projects.

  • W3C Government Linked Data (GLD) Working Group
    James McKinney, invited expert

    The group develops open standards that help governments publish their data as effective and usable linked data, using semantic web technologies, including the Data Catalog Vocabulary (DCAT) and Organization Ontology (ORG).

  • Open Civic Data

    The Sunlight Foundation is developing a solution to uniquely identify political divisions, as well as developing JSON schema for bills, events and votes. You may participate through their mailing list.

  • Open Knowledge Foundation

    The OKF is developing a variety of data protocols and formats for working with data (mailing list and repository), including a Data Catalog Interoperability Protocol (mailing list and repository). Its Open Spending working group is developing a specification for Transaction-Level Spending Data (mailing list and repository).

  • OpenElections

    This Knight Foundation-funded project's goal is to create a comprehensive, open database of historical election results in the United States (mailing list and repository).

  • Code for America

    Code for America has been involved in recent data specification projects, including House Facts and Yelp's Local Inspector Value-Entry Specification (LIVES). You can discuss the specifications on the Municipal Innovation list.

Event Organizing

Open North organizes and hosts events and workshops that bring people together to learn about open data and create tools for open government. We regularly organize small thematic get-togethers for local open data communities to meet, chat and make progress on their projects.

  • EcoHack October 19, 2013
    Stéphane Guidoin, co-organizer

    Montreal's first green-themed hackathon where nonprofits, community groups, municipal employees and students can collaborate to build a more environmentally sustainable city.

  • Open Data Day Montreal February 23, 2013
    Stéphane Guidoin, co-organizer

    As part of International Open Data Day, Open North co-organized a one-day conference and hackathon, welcoming over 180 participants including developers, designers, politicians and civil servants. To mark the event, the City of Montreal released 21 new datasets. Presenters included members of the executive branches of the Government of Quebec and the City of Montreal.

  • Hackons La Corruption November 10-11, 2013
    Co-organizer

    The two-day conference and hackathon brought together 200 developers, designers, activists, and public servants from across the province to leverage information technology and open data to collaboratively design tools to prevent corruption and collusion. Presenters covered the causes of corruption, public sector ethics, and tools for transparency among other topics.

  • HackTaVille September 8, 2012
    Stéphane Guidoin, co-organizer and workshop leader

    The hackathon focused on the themes of transportation, urban planning and public spaces and produced several open data apps. A full day of workshops ran alongside the hackathon. The roughly 150 participants came from a wide range of backgrounds and experience, from experienced developers to freshman urban planning students.

  • Open Data Hack Day December 10, 2011
    James McKinney, co-organizer

    Thanks to eight volunteers, we launched an alpha version of Represent, which allowed citizens to find their elected officials at the federal and provincial levels of government by address or postal code.

Technology & Policy

Open North advocates for open data, transparency and accountability in government. We advise governments, businesses, nonprofits and local groups on how to lobby for, implement and use open data. To get in touch, contact info@opennorth.ca.

  • Treasury Board of Canada Open Data round table May 10, 2013, Montreal
    Stéphane Guidoin

    Part of a series of cross-country consultations with experts in the open data community focusing on which new features and data to prioritize for the re-launch of the federal government's Open Data Portal.

  • Treasury Board of Canada Open Data round table March 11, 2013, Toronto
    Ellie Marshall

    Part of a series of cross-country consultations with experts in the open data community focusing on which new features and data to prioritize for the re-launch of the federal government's Open Data Portal.

  • Technology for Transparent and Accountable Public Finance report May 30, 2012

    The Open Knowledge Foundation featured our work on increasing public understanding of government spending and increasing participation in the budgeting process in its report for the Global Initiative on Fiscal Transparency.

  • Table de concertation sur les données ouvertes de Montréal April 25, 2012–
    Stéphane Guidoin

    The Table de concertation, composed of six civil servants of the city and six members of civil society, meets five times per year to identify and prioritize datasets for release and to discuss improvements to the city's open data portal, its license and its relationship with the community.
