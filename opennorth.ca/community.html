Event Organizing

Open North is a leader in the civic technology sector. We organize and host events that bring together like-minded groups to share best practices and create open government tools. We also regularly organize small thematic get-togethers for local open data communities to meet, chat and make progress on their projects.

  • EcoHack October 19, 2013
    Stéphane Guidoin, co-organizer

    Montreal's first green-themed hackathon where nonprofits, community groups, municipal employees and students can collaborate to transform the city. No technical experience or knowledge of urban sustainability required - just an interest in a more sustainable city!

  • Open Data Day Montreal February 23, 2013
    Stéphane Guidoin, co-organizer

    As part of International Open Data Day 2013, Open North co-organized this hackathon and conference with Montréal Ouvert and Québec Ouvert and hosted over 170 participants.

  • Hackons La Corruption November 10-11, 2013
    Co-organizer

    A two-day hackathon dedicated to solving corruption in Montreal using open data.

  • HackTaVille September 8, 2012
    Stéphane Guidoin, co-organizer and workshop leader

    Co-organized with Ecole Polytechnique de Montréal, the Student Society of McGill University and Living Lab Montréal, the hackathon focused on the themes of transportation, urban planning and public spaces which resulted in the creation of six open data projects.

  • Open Data Hack Day December 10, 2011
    James McKinney, co-organizer

    With the help of eight volunteers, we were able to launch an alpha version of Represent, allowing users to find elected officials at the federal and provincial levels by address or postal code.

    Standards Development

    Open North is dedicated to establishing and maintaining open data standards which increase access to government information and promote interoperability. We are experienced community leaders and have developed two open standards, Popolo and Open511. We also particpate in the maintenance of other standards.

    • W3C Government Linked Data (GLD) Working Group
      James McKinney

      The GLD group works to develop standards which help governments publish their data as effective and usable Linked Data, using Semantic Web technologies. Open North's Executive Director, James McKinney, joined this working group as an invited expert.

    Technology & Policy

    Open North advocates for the adoption open government practices and initiatives. For example, our feedback was solicited by the Government of Canada on their Open Data Portal and we submitted to the Global Intiative for Fiscal Transparency (GIFT) report, "Technology for Transparent and Accountable Public Finance". We also advise local groups in the open data community. To connect with our experts, please email us at info@opennorth.ca.

    Experiments

    The following projects were developed by our team as tests or prototypes as we work towards increasing access to government information and creating meaningful civic engagement. You can follow our development work on GitHub.

    Multimodal pour Montréal is a trip planner for Montreal, started by Nicolas Saunier and Guillaume Barreau and completed by Open North. It supports combinations of public transit, walking, cycling, and bike-share. This project re-uses the open source OpenTripPlanner platform by OpenPlans.

    VéloPlan is a bike trip planner that lets you prioritize safety, speed or flatness to find the best cycling routes in Montreal. It integrates the docking stations of the Bixi public bike-share program, making it easy to find the nearest docks. VéloPlan re-uses the Cibi.me open source software by OpenPlans.

    Yul311 is a prototype for reporting local problems online in Montreal, using Open311, an open standard for issue tracking, and re-using uReport and Open311 Proxy by the City of Bloomington. This project was developed as an alternative to the 3-1-1 phone number used by many Canadian cities to provide easy access to non-emergency services and to report problems like potholes and graffiti.
