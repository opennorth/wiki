Citizen Budget

Citizen Budget is a powerful online tool to involve residents in decision-making processes and to demonstrate a municipality's commitment to citizen engagement. The interactive budget simulator allows the public to consult and modify municipal service budgets and submit realistic, balanced proposals that express their budget priorities. First launched in the Plateau Mont-Royal borough of Montreal, QC in 2011, Citizen Budget has been deployed by municipalities across North America.

Open 511

Open 511 is an open standard and framework for managing and publishing road events, funded by the Government of Canada. It will be a suite of products to help governments and citizens better handle disruptive events in the road system, such as road work, hazards, accidents and closures. The API is currently available as a draft.

MyCityHall.ca/MaMairie.ca

MyCityHall.ca MaMairie.ca is a resource for tracking the activity of Montreal officials. The platform is currently being developed into an open standard so that civic-minded developers around the world can adapt it to track their local government. It will be relaunched as MyCityHall.ca in summer 2013, with the addition of Toronto and Ottawa. The platform will allow citizens to track the activity of local elected officials, motions, agendas, and give citizens an opportunity to ask questions, and discuss events from city hall.

Represent

Represent is an open database of elected representatives and administrative boundaries for the federal, provincial and municipal levels of government in Canada.Represent is deployed by over a dozen civil society organizations to connect members and volunteers to their representatives. Represent uses only authoritative government data sources for electoral boundaries and elected officials. We are in regular contact with governments to ensure our electoral boundaries are always up-to-date.

Other Contributions

We publish our open-source projects on GitHub. We regularly contribute to other organization’s open-source projects, such as OpenCorporates, an open database of the world's companies, and Alaveteli, software for making freedom of information requests.


Past Initiatives

Resto-Net

In 2011, Montrealers browsed over 700 000 pages on Resto-Net.ca to check if a local restaurant had recently violated a health code. Launched November 2010.

Patiner Montréal

PatinerMontreal.ca provides up-to-date information on the condition of outdoor rinks in the City of Montreal. Since its launch in 2010, over 24 000 skaters have used the website to plan their winter fun. The website publishes its data through an API, which developers have used to create Android versions. Launched December 2010.

Gazette Documents

Gazette Documents is an online database of contracts awarded by the City of Montreal. Built for the Montreal Gazette, the project extracts contracts data from hundreds of PDFs so that journalists and citizens can explore and analyze how public money is spent. To date, at least three articles at two newspapers have been written using this database. Launched September 2011.

Decision Canada

Open North created the “Get to Know Your Candidates” interactive feature for the Montreal Gazette, with photos, social media links, and other basic information for all candidates in the 2011 federal election. Launched April 2011.
